📖 What These Results Mean

This calculator compares your current mortgage payment to a new refinanced one, showing your monthly savings and how many months it takes to recoup your closing costs (the “break-even point”). If you plan to stay in the home longer than the break-even period, refinancing is usually worth it. If you'll move sooner, the closing costs may outweigh the savings.

When to Refinance

If your new rate is at least 0.75% lower and you plan to stay 3+ years, refinancing likely makes financial sense. Calculate your break-even point first.

What Are Closing Costs?

Typically 2–5% of the loan amount ($6k–$15k on a $300k loan). Your break-even shows when monthly savings pay those back.

💡 Tip: Multiple mortgage inquiries within 45 days count as one credit pull. Shop at least 3 lenders for the best refinance rate.
