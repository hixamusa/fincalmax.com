What Is an Escrow Account and How Does It Work?

Most homeowners pay into an escrow account every month without fully understanding it. Here is a clear explanation.

Advertisement · 728×90

What Is Escrow?

An escrow account is managed by your lender. Each month, part of your payment goes into escrow. When property taxes and insurance bills come due, the lender pays them automatically from this account.

Why Lenders Require It

To protect their collateral. If you fail to pay property taxes, the government can seize your home. Escrow ensures these critical bills always get paid.

📊 Example: Annual taxes $4,800 + Insurance $1,200 = $6,000 per year divided by 12 = $500 added to your monthly payment for escrow.

Escrow Analysis

Lenders review your escrow annually. If taxes or insurance increased, your payment goes up. If there is a surplus, you get a refund. These adjustments are normal.

Bottom Line

Escrow simplifies homeownership by spreading annual bills into monthly payments. It is not extra cost — it is organized budgeting. Use our Mortgage Calculator to include escrow in your estimates.
