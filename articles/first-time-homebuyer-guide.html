First-Time Homebuyer Guide 2026: Everything You Need to Know

A complete step-by-step guide for first-time homebuyers in 2026. From saving your down payment to getting your keys.

Advertisement · 728×90

Step 1: Check Your Financial Health

Review your credit score, monthly income, existing debts, and savings. Aim for a credit score above 680, DTI below 36%, and at least 3% of the home price saved. Use our Affordability Calculator to find your budget.

Step 2: Get Pre-Approved

Pre-approval is a lender commitment to loan you up to a certain amount. It requires full documentation and a credit check. Sellers prefer buyers with pre-approval letters.

Step 3: House Hunt Strategically

Visit homes in person — photos always lie. Check the neighborhood at different times. Look at comparable sales before making any offer.

Step 4: Make an Offer

Your agent will help craft a competitive offer. Always include an inspection contingency. Never skip the home inspection to save money.

Step 5: Close the Deal

Closing takes 30–60 days. Budget 2%–5% of the loan for closing costs on top of your down payment. Use our Closing Costs Calculator to estimate.

Bottom Line

The home buying process is a marathon. Take it step by step and use our calculators to know your numbers at every stage.
