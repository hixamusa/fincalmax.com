Should You Refinance Your Mortgage in 2026?

With rates shifting throughout 2026, millions of homeowners are wondering if now is the right time to refinance. The answer depends on your specific numbers.

Advertisement · 728×90

The Break-Even Point Rule

Refinancing costs money upfront — typically 2%–5% of the loan. The break-even point is how many months until your monthly savings cover those costs. Use our Refinance Calculator to find yours.

📊 Example: $6,000 closing costs ÷ $200/month savings = 30 months to break even. If you plan to stay 30+ months, refinancing makes sense.

When Refinancing Makes Sense

Refinance when your new rate is at least 0.75% lower, you plan to stay 3+ years, and your break-even is under 24 months. Also consider refinancing if your credit score improved significantly or you want to switch from ARM to fixed.

When NOT to Refinance

Skip refinancing if you plan to sell within 2 years, you are late in your loan term (most interest already paid), or the rate difference is under 0.5%.

Bottom Line

Do not refinance based on headlines. Run your personal numbers with our Refinance Calculator and make a data-driven decision.
