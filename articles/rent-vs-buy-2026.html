Rent vs. Buy in 2026: The Honest Financial Comparison

With elevated rates and high prices, the rent vs. buy debate has never been more complex. Here is an honest look at both sides.

Advertisement · 728×90

The True Cost of Buying

Most buyers focus only on the mortgage payment. The true monthly cost includes principal and interest, property taxes, homeowners insurance, maintenance (budget 1% of value per year), HOA fees, and PMI if applicable. Use our Mortgage Calculator to estimate your full cost.

📊 On a $450k home, true ownership cost is often $1,000–$1,500 more than the mortgage payment alone.

The Hidden Advantage of Buying: Equity

Every payment builds equity in an asset that historically appreciates 4–5% per year. Renters build zero equity and face annual rent increases.

When Renting Makes More Sense

You plan to move within 3–5 years, your local price-to-rent ratio is above 25, or you value flexibility.

When Buying Makes More Sense

You plan to stay 5+ years, monthly ownership cost is within 15–20% of comparable rent, and you have a stable income and emergency fund.
