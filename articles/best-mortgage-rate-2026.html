How to Get the Best Mortgage Rate in 2026

Your credit score, down payment, and loan type determine your rate. Here's exactly how to get the lowest possible mortgage rate in 2026.

Advertisement · 728×90

1. Your Credit Score Is Everything

Lenders use your credit score to decide risk. The higher your score, the lower your rate. Borrowers above 760 get the best rates available — typically 0.5%–1% lower than someone at 680.

💡 Check your credit report free at AnnualCreditReport.com. Dispute any errors before applying — even small corrections can improve your score significantly.

2. Save a Bigger Down Payment

A larger down payment reduces your loan-to-value ratio. The magic threshold is 20% — at that point, you avoid PMI and qualify for better rates. Use our Down Payment Calculator to plan your savings.

3. Shop At Least 3–5 Lenders

Studies show getting just one extra quote saves the average buyer $1,500 over the loan life. Compare credit unions, online lenders, and mortgage brokers — not just your bank.

4. Choose the Right Loan Type

Conventional loans are best for buyers with 20%+ down and strong credit. FHA loans have lower credit requirements but require mortgage insurance. VA loans offer the best rates but require military service.

5. Lock Your Rate

Once under contract, ask about a rate lock. This freezes your rate for 30–60 days while closing is finalized. If rates rise, you are protected.

Bottom Line

Prepare 6–12 months before buying. Improve your score, save 20% if possible, and compare multiple lenders. Use our Mortgage Calculator to see how different rates affect your payment.
