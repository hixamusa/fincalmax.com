Home Inspection Guide: What to Look For Before You Buy

A home inspection protects you from buying someone elses problems. A 500 dollar inspection can reveal 30000 dollars in hidden issues.

Advertisement · 728×90

What Does a Home Inspection Cover?

Roof condition and age, foundation integrity, electrical system, plumbing and water pressure, HVAC systems, insulation, windows and doors, and signs of water damage or mold.

Red Flags to Watch For

Water stains on ceilings (active or past leaks), foundation cracks wider than 1/4 inch, knob-and-tube wiring (fire hazard), polybutylene pipes (known to fail), HVAC older than 15 years, and roof older than 20 years.

💡 Never waive the inspection contingency — even in competitive markets. Use findings to negotiate price reductions or repairs.

Specialty Inspections Worth Considering

Radon test ($150), sewer scope for older homes ($200), and mold inspection if water damage is visible ($300–$500).

Bottom Line

Always attend the inspection. A good inspector earns their fee many times over. Never skip this step to save time or money.
