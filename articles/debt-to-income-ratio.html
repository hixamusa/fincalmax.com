Debt-to-Income Ratio: What Lenders Look For in 2026

Your DTI ratio determines whether you qualify for a mortgage and at what rate. Here is how to calculate yours and improve it before applying.

Advertisement · 728×90

What Is DTI?

DTI is your total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income. Lenders use it to assess your ability to manage another payment. Check yours with our DTI Calculator.

DTILender View
Under 28%Excellent
28–36%Good
36–43%Acceptable
Above 43%Risky — may be denied

How to Lower Your DTI

Pay off debt before applying (especially high-balance credit cards), avoid new debt 6–12 months before applying, and increase your income if possible.

Bottom Line

Lenders want your housing costs below 28% of income and total debt below 36–43%. Get your DTI in order 6–12 months before applying.
