How We Calculate

Transparency is core to our mission. Below are the exact formulas we use in every FincalMax calculator — the same formulas used by banks, mortgage lenders, and financial institutions worldwide.

1. Monthly Mortgage Payment

We use the standard mortgage amortization formula, which is the industry standard used by all US lenders:

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1] Where: M = Monthly payment P = Principal loan amount r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12) n = Number of payments (years × 12)

Example: $320,000 loan at 6.85% for 30 years:
r = 6.85% ÷ 12 = 0.5708%, n = 360
M = $320,000 × [0.005708 × (1.005708)³⁶⁰] / [(1.005708)³⁶⁰ - 1] = $2,107/month

2. Total Monthly Payment (PITI)

Our mortgage calculator shows the true monthly cost, not just principal and interest:

Total = P&I + (Annual Property Tax ÷ 12) + (Annual Insurance ÷ 12) + PMI

3. PMI Calculation

Private Mortgage Insurance is required when LTV exceeds 80%:

PMI Monthly = (Loan Amount × PMI Rate) ÷ 12 PMI Rate varies by lender: typically 0.3%–1.5% We use 0.8% as our default estimate

4. Refinance Savings

Monthly Savings = Current Payment - New Payment Break-Even (months) = Closing Costs ÷ Monthly Savings Total Savings = (Monthly Savings × New Loan Term in months) - Closing Costs

5. Debt-to-Income Ratio

DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income) × 100

6. Home Equity

Equity = Current Home Value - Outstanding Loan Balance LTV = (Outstanding Balance ÷ Current Value) × 100

7. Loan-to-Value (LTV)

LTV = (Loan Amount ÷ Property Value) × 100

Data Sources

  • Default interest rates sourced from Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey
  • Property tax defaults based on US Census Bureau national averages
  • Insurance defaults based on Insurance Information Institute data
  • PMI rates based on Mortgage Bankers Association guidelines
