📖 What These Results Mean

FHA loans let you buy with as little as 3.5% down, but they charge mortgage insurance (MIP) that regular loans don't always require. This calculator adds the upfront MIP (1.75%, rolled into your loan) and the ongoing monthly MIP on top of your principal & interest. Compare the “Total Monthly Payment” here against a conventional loan with PMI to see which costs less for your situation.

Formula Used

FHA monthly payment = Principal & Interest + Upfront MIP (1.75% financed) + Annual MIP (0.55%–1.05% ÷ 12). The MIP cost makes FHA more expensive long-term than conventional for buyers with strong credit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an FHA loan? +
An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration. It allows borrowers with lower credit scores (580+) and smaller down payments (3.5%) to qualify for a home loan.
What is the FHA loan limit in 2026? +
The FHA loan limit for most US counties in 2026 is $524,225 for a single-family home. High-cost areas like San Francisco and New York have higher limits up to $1,209,750.
What credit score do I need for an FHA loan? +
A minimum 580 credit score qualifies you for 3.5% down. Scores between 500-579 require 10% down. Below 500, FHA loans are not available.
Does FHA require mortgage insurance? +
Yes. FHA requires both an upfront MIP (1.75% of loan) and annual MIP (0.55%-1.05%). If you put less than 10% down, MIP lasts the life of the loan.
