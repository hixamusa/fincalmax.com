📖 What These Results Mean

Your take-home pay is your salary minus federal income tax, FICA (Social Security + Medicare), and any pre-tax deductions like 401(k) contributions or health insurance premiums. Those pre-tax deductions actually lower your taxable income, which is part of why maxing out a 401(k) costs you less in take-home pay than the contribution amount itself. Remember: this estimate excludes state income tax, which can take another 0-13% depending on where you live.
